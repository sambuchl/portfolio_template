Building with deliberate speed

How to keep standards high while maintaining the momentum that ambitious products demand.

Speed is the most under-valued competitive advantage. Teams that compound small decisions quickly unlock more surface area to learn, iterate, and ultimately ship the work that matters. But moving fast without a clear bar for quality is just as dangerous as moving slowly.

Over the past decade I’ve experimented with a handful of rituals that help me stay close to the work while keeping standards uncompromisingly high. The principles here are portable—you can apply them to a two-person startup or a hundred-person product org.

1. Define quality in plain language

Every project deserves a short description of what “great” looks like. If the outcome can’t be captured in a paragraph, the scope is probably too big. Document the must-haves, the nice-to-haves, and the non-goals. Revisit that description every week.

Momentum without clarity creates fragility. The fastest teams I know make sure everyone can articulate the standard in one breath.

2. Ship in narrative form

Before we cut code or design flows, I ask the owner to write a few paragraphs describing the finished experience. Writing exposes fuzzy thinking in a way that checklists never do.

3. Close the loop with customers every week

A single conversation with a customer is worth a dozen debates. Build a habit of meeting someone who recently used what you built. Ask them to walk through the moments of friction. Capture their words verbatim.

These rhythms keep the team anchored to reality. They also protect morale—when you know why a decision exists, you’re far more likely to find the energy to improve it.
